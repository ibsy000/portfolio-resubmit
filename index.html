The Reason I'm Here

Dedicated to my brother; my inspiration and every supportive loved one in my life.

Growing up, my brother was always the techy one. He always wanted the newest gaming console or he was learning about computers or he would take electronics apart and put them back together. I was right there beside him interested in what he was doing or I was playing video games with him. When I got a little older, my brother passed down an old thick, heavy laptop to me. He taught me how to use emulators to play old games for the N64, NES, GameBoy, GameCube, and so on. I loved that laptop! (I still have it somewhere!) When I was old enough to get a job in high school I would save up my wages to buy my own consoles. We started to compete with each other on who would get the newest console first. We actually still do that to this day. As we got older, my brother started to get really into gaming computers. He started to learn all the ins and outs of what makes a computer function. It was like he was speaking a different language! At this point, I was focused on schoolwork, work work, and extracurricular activities. We didn't see each other very often then because we had our own things going on.

>>Fast forward a few years. At this point, I am engaged to my best-est friend, we own a home, have three fur-babies, and have stable jobs. I think to myself, I should look into gaming computers! It's time to level up my gaming! I got some advice from my brother and did some of my own research as well. I decided to buy a budget-friendly-VR-capable gaming computer and a VR setup because I had to 1-UP my brother, of course. I found my new passion! Not only did I enjoy the entire gaming aspect, but I also spent endless hours going through every directory, every setting, researching components, always learning. I have fun trying to understand how things work. I utilized my knowledge in my jobs and was always helpful when there were new updates, software, applications, computers, and so on.

>>>Fast fast forward to this year. My fiance and I just finished remodeling our house earlier this year. We also just recently lost two of our dogs, which was really tough for both of us. We also had truck problems and a well that decided to quit working. We were at a point in our lives where we wanted to make a change. We didn't feel fulfilled in the area we lived in or with our jobs. At this point, the housing market is so high we decided to sell our house and retire for a while until the market drops to purchase or build a new home in a new area. We wanted to travel after selling our house but the gas prices also skyrocketed. We don't want to blow through our profits on gas, that's no fun. Family offered their home to us until we can find a rental property in the meantime. During my free time I was thinking about what my next career path would be. Then I stumbled upon the answer! I saw an advertisement online for Career Karma and my first thoughts were, This is a scam. This has got to be a scam. Go to a bootcamp and get certified in a tech field in less than a year? I have to see if this is legit.

After some research, I came to the conclusion that it's not a scam. I signed up, attended a few live sessions explaining bootcamps and the different tech career paths. I felt like this is exactly what I have been looking for. I talked to my fiance first to see what he thinks about it and he supports me 100%! I began researching bootcamps to find the right one for me. After looking at a few options, I found Coding Temple. This school stood out right away. They are transparent about what they have to offer throughout the entire website. By the end of reviewing their page, I only had one question, Where do I sign up? The enrollment process was so easy, quick, and streamlined.

I explained my plans to my family, my fiance's family, and my friends. My brother says to me, Welcome to the nerd club. They have all been super supportive and have been cheering me on for success! I am so excited about learning everything I can about software engineering in this cohort. So far, the pre-work has been a little overwhelming and stressful, but ultimately it's been really fun for me. I never want to stop learning! After this cohort, I plan on getting a job in the industry for a while and continue learning new tech paths. My ultimate goal is to learn how to make my own video games and continue to do that until I'm ready to really retire!
